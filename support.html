Quick help

💬
Companion not replying?

Replies are generated live and take a few seconds. If it hangs for more than a minute, check your connection, then close and reopen the app — your conversation is saved. The skills, diary card, and crisis kit all work offline.

⭐
Restore Quietoak Plus?

Open the Plus screen and tap Restore Purchases. If your Apple ID has an active subscription, it re-links in seconds.

🪶
Hit the free chat limit?

Companion chat has a gentle daily limit that resets each day — it's generous, so most people never reach it, and crisis chat is never limited. The skills library, diary card, and the crisis kit are always free.

🎙️
Voice dictation isn't working?

Diary dictation needs microphone permission: iOS Settings → Quietoak → Microphone. The audio is sent to our backend and on to OpenAI only to produce the text, then discarded — it isn't stored.

🐛
Found a bug?

Email hi@quietoak.app with what you saw and what you were doing. Screenshots help — please avoid including anything private from your diary or chat.

💸
Refund or billing?

All purchases are handled by Apple. Request refunds via reportaproblem.apple.com. Manage or cancel Plus in iOS Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.

For clinicians, reviewers & press

If you're a DBT-informed clinician, a reviewer, or a journalist evaluating Quietoak, reach out at hi@quietoak.app. Quietoak is a self-help adjunct based on the Linehan DBT model — not therapy — with prominent disclaimers, an always-free crisis kit, and an independent crisis-safety layer.

Where your data goes

The full data flow is in our privacy policy — short version: your diary and practice stay on your device, there's no account, no ads, and no third-party trackers on your diary or chat; your companion messages are sent to an AI provider to generate replies and, under that provider's current API terms, aren't used to train their models.
